Quantum Tunneling in Curved Spacetime: A New Approach

We present a novel framework for analyzing quantum tunneling phenomena in strongly curved spacetime, extending the semiclassical WKB approximation to non-trivial topologies and dynamic metrics. Our approach leverages path integral methods and introduces a new class of instanton solutions relevant for black hole event horizons and early-universe cosmology. We discuss implications for Hawking radiation, vacuum decay, and the information paradox, providing both analytic and numerical results. This work opens new avenues for connecting quantum field theory, general relativity, and observable signatures in astrophysical phenomena.

Expedition: The Night the Mountain Glowed

It was just past midnight when the first aurora flickered above the ridge. I was alone, save for the hum of my portable detector and the distant echo of glacial runoff. The air was thin, electric, and every so often the Geiger counter would chirp, reminding me of the cosmic rays that never sleep. I set up my camera, hoping to capture the interplay of light and ionization, but what I saw instead was a faint, blue glow emanating from the rocks themselves.
